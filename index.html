Bootcamp Journal

About

This is a journal website documenting what I've learnt while on the Growth Company Software Developer Skills Bootcamp.

I talk about the different skills I've learnt, my progression as the weeks have passed, and the various pitfalls and hurdles I've hit and learnt lessons from.

I started the course with a small amount of experience with HTML, CSS and JavaScript after following tutorials on w3Schools.com and taking a short intro to web development course. Over the 11 weeks on the bootcamp I've learnt a whole lot and am all the more fascinated and enraptured to keep learning.

I made this homepage here on week 2 of the 11 week bootcamp as part of a submission to showcase what we'd learnt on HTML, CSS and JS during the beginning of the course. The brief was to make a site where we made journal entries talking about what we had learnt and achieved.

As I carried on adding more to the site through the weeks it transitioned into more of a revision tool for myself where I could write up my notes and come back to them. Hopefully it still proves useful for presenting the knowledge I obtained throughout the course. You can see more journal style entries of what I've been up to on my portfolio.

Sum By Factors

I wanted to try a 4 kyu Kata for a challenge and found this one called Sum By Factors.
The goal was, given an array of integers, to produce a sorted array of all prime factors and the sum of their multiples, in ascending order.

Various 7+kyu's invloving arrays

When I can I've been trying to get a 7kyu in a day. Loving the challenge and they're great for imporving my JS skills and learning from other developers.

Plants and Zombies

After completing Sum By Factors I've gone for another challenge and upped the difficulty to 3kyu this time. This Kata is a fun Sim-like challenge based on the PlantsVsZombies game. You have to calculate how many moves any given situation will last before the game ends.

Drumkit Proj

This was a set project as part of the course in order to implement and consolidate what we'd researched on keyboard keycodes and how they can be used with Js DOM.
The webpage plays an audio file of a certain piece of kit dependant on what key you press🥁

Shuffle Cards Js Proj

Just had an idea to try writing a JS program that simulates shuffling a deck of cards to practice JS fundamentals. Did that and coded a test to test that it's accurate.

After that I thought it would be cool to apply it and maybe make a site that you can play different card games on to further test my JS and web dev skills. Made the beginnings of a texas holdem sim using Js DOM commands and functions.
